Casabe(Cassava bread)

Casabe is a flatbread made from cassava, a staple food in the Cuban diet.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg cassava.
  • ¼ teaspoon salt.
  • 1 teaspoon mild-flavored oil.

Instructions

  1. Peel and thoroughly wash the cassava, then cut it in half and remove the fibrous center.
  2. Next, grate it using the finest side of a grater.
  3. Once grated, add salt, place the mixture onto a clean cotton cloth, wrap it up, and squeeze to extract all the liquid.
  4. Then, spread the remaining pulp onto a flat tray and place it in the refrigerator for 4 hours, stirring every hour to break it up and ensure the texture is as fine as possible.
  5. After 4 hours, it should be slightly damp; now it is time to cook it to make the "casabe" (cassava flatbread).
  6. Place a skillet over medium-high heat and add oil, spreading it across the entire surface; this is simply to prevent the grated cassava from sticking during cooking.
  7. Add 2 or 3 spoonfuls of cassava to the skillet and flatten it with the spoon to create a thin "casabe" patty.
  8. Cook for 1 minute on each side, or until golden brown, the remaining moisture has evaporated, and the mixture breaks apart easily. Remember that "casabe" should have a crispy texture; if it is still somewhat pliable, it needs more cooking time.
  9. And that is how easy it is to make homemade "casabe"; now you can enjoy it whenever you like. Store it in a dry place to maintain its crispy texture.
