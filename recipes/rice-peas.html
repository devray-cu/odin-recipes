Moros y cristianos(Rice and peas)

Its origins date back to the colonial era, when enslaved Africans and Spanish colonists shared dishes on the island. The combination of rice and black beans symbolizes the harmonious coexistence of both cultures at the table.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. rice
  • 1 lb. black beans
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 large chili pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ tsp. oregano
  • ½ tsp. cumin
  • 4 tbsp. oil
  • 4 tbsp. lard
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 tbsp. vinegar

Instructions

  1. The beans must be soaked overnight.
  2. The next day, place them in a pot with half the salt, the bay leaf, and one of the chili peppers.
  3. Cover and cook until tender.
  4. Drain the beans; if the cooking liquid measures less than a liter, add enough water to reach that amount.
  5. Sauté the onion and chili pepper in a pan with hot oil.
  6. Then add half the garlic, crushed together with the oregano and cumin (which should be toasted beforehand).
  7. Add the beans and the rinsed rice, along with the reserved bean cooking liquid and the remaining salt.
  8. Cook over high heat until boiling, then reduce to medium heat and cook until the rice is tender and the grains are separate.
  9. Add the vinegar.
  10. Separately, sauté the remaining garlic in lard until golden; remove the garlic and gradually add the fat to the rice, mixing well.
  11. If desired, pork trimmings may be used by following the same procedure described in the recipe.
